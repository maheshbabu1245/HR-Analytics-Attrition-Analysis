# HR Analytics Dashboard - Attrition Analysis

## Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition and workforce trends using Power BI. 
The dashboard helps HR teams understand the major factors affecting employee turnover and
supports data-driven decision-making.

---

## Tools Used
- Power BI
- Excel
- Data Visualization
- Data Analysis

---

## Business Problem
Organizations often face challenges in understanding why employees leave the company. This dashboard helps identify key attrition factors based on age group, salary, department, job role, education, marital status, and business travel.

---

## Dashboard Features
- Employee Count KPI
- Attrition Count & Attrition Rate
- Average Salary & Average Tenure
- Attrition Analysis by Age Group
- Department-wise Attrition
- Salary Slab Analysis
- Education-wise Attrition
- Business Travel Analysis
- Interactive Filters and Slicers

---

## Key Insights
- Overall attrition rate is 18.99%.
- Employees aged 26-35 show the highest attrition.
- Research & Development department has maximum attrition.
- Employees with lower salary slabs have higher resignation rates.
- Frequent business travelers show higher attrition compared to non-travel employees.
- Single employees contribute more to attrition than married employees.

---

## Business Impact
This dashboard helps HR departments identify employee retention challenges, 
improve workforce planning, and make better strategic decisions using data insights.
